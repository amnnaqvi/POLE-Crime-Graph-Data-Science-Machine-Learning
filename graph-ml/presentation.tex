\begin{frame}{Problem and Contribution}
  \textbf{Question:} How can a POLE crime graph help investigators decide where to look next, without turning weak model scores into accusations?

  \vspace{0.25cm}
  \begin{columns}[T]
    \column{0.48\textwidth}
      \textbf{What we built}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Neo4j graph model for people, crimes, locations, phones, emails, vehicles, and officers.
        \item Graph statistics and analytics for scale, hotspots, communities, and review priority.
        \item GDS link prediction for possible missing social-family associations.
      \end{itemize}

    \column{0.48\textwidth}
      \textbf{Responsible stance}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Use dense evidence for hotspot and community insight.
        \item Keep \tinycode{PARTY\_TO} as context, not an automated target.
        \item Write only review-only social candidates, never person-crime claims.
      \end{itemize}
  \end{columns}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Why the Target Changed}
  \small
  \begin{table}
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{p{0.22\textwidth}r p{0.50\textwidth}}
      \toprule
      \textbf{Link family} & \textbf{Links} & \textbf{Final reading} \\
      \midrule
      Crime $\rightarrow$ Location & 28,762 & Best for hotspot analysis because every crime has a place. \\
      Person $\rightarrow$ Person & 1,180 raw links & Best for communities and social-link prediction. \\
      Vehicle $\rightarrow$ Crime & 978 & Useful supporting context, but mostly one-off. \\
      Person $\rightarrow$ Location & 368 & Useful exposure context, not proof. \\
      Person $\rightarrow$ Crime & 55 & Too sparse and sensitive for the main ML target. \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
  \end{table}

  \vspace{0.1cm}
  Direct person-crime prediction had only 55 observed \tinycode{PARTY\_TO} links out of 10,613,178 possible person-crime pairs, or 0.0005\%. So the final ML target became missing \tinycode{Person--Person} social-family links.
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{GML Task Design}
  \small
  \textbf{Final GML task:} link prediction for possible missing social-family relationships between people.

  \vspace{0.16cm}
  \begin{table}
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{p{0.24\textwidth}p{0.64\textwidth}}
      \toprule
      \textbf{Stage} & \textbf{Project choice} \\
      \midrule
      Input graph & Person, Phone, Email, Location, and Crime nodes with social, contact, address, \tinycode{PARTY\_TO}, and \tinycode{OCCURRED\_AT} context. \\
      Target & 586 unique observed social-family person pairs, sampled against non-linked pairs. \\
      Features & FastRP 128D and Node2Vec 64D embeddings; Hadamard product combines two node vectors into pair features. \\
      Split & 60\% training, 20\% held-out test, 3-fold validation inside training, negative sampling ratio 1.0. \\
      Model & Random Forest; Logistic Regression was tested but produced flatter live probabilities. \\
      Output & Ranked \tinycode{Person--Person} association candidates for human review only. \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
  \end{table}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[fragile]{Main Training Query}
  \small
  Building supervised model is actually built.

  \vspace{0.12cm}
  \begin{verbatim}
CALL gds.beta.pipeline.linkPrediction.train(
  'socialFamilyContextGraph',
  {
    pipeline: 'social-family-lp-pipeline',
    modelName: 'social-family-lp-model',
    sourceNodeLabel: 'Person',
    targetNodeLabel: 'Person',
    targetRelationshipType: 'OBSERVED_SOCIAL_LINK_TMP',
    metrics: ['AUCPR'],
    randomSeed: 42
  }
)
YIELD modelInfo, trainMillis
RETURN modelInfo.metrics.AUCPR.train.avg AS trainAUCPR,
       modelInfo.metrics.AUCPR.validation.avg AS validationAUCPR,
       modelInfo.metrics.AUCPR.test AS testAUCPR,
       modelInfo.bestParameters.methodName AS selectedModel;
  \end{verbatim}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Supervised vs Explainable LP}
  \small
  \begin{table}
    \centering
    \begin{tabular}{p{0.26\textwidth}p{0.30\textwidth}p{0.32\textwidth}}
      \toprule
      \textbf{Approach} & \textbf{How it works} & \textbf{Project use} \\
      \midrule
      Supervised LP & Learns from known social-family links using embeddings and Random Forest. Outputs probabilities for unseen person pairs. & Good for ranking many candidates, but the explanation is indirect and needs a deployment gate. \\
      Common Neighbours & Counts mutual contacts between two people with no existing social edge. & Very explainable; suggests a link because specific shared people connect them. \\
      Adamic Adar & Like Common Neighbours, but rare shared contacts get more weight than very common contacts. & Final high-specificity explainable write-back: 4 review-only links. \\
      \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
  \end{table}

  \vspace{0.12cm}
  Supervised LP answers ``how likely?'' Explainable LP answers ``why should a reviewer look at this pair?''
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Limitations, Ethics, and Next Steps}
  \small
  \begin{columns}[T]
    \column{0.48\textwidth}
      \textbf{Limitations and ethics}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Sparse labels make direct person-crime prediction unreliable.
        \item Historical hotspots are not causal forecasts.
        \item Address exposure and social proximity are context only.
        \item No automated crime claims are written.
      \end{itemize}

    \column{0.48\textwidth}
      \textbf{Improvements}
      \begin{itemize}
        \item Add time-aware splits to reduce leakage risk.
        \item Collect better labels for social and investigative links.
        \item Compare more seeds, split ratios, thresholds, and model families.
        \item Require explainable evidence before any person-level write-back.
      \end{itemize}
  \end{columns}
\end{frame}
